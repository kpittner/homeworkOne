Quick Facts
Ribbit

Fire bellied toads thrive in wet, wooded areas. They love to hang out in ponds or slow moving streams.

When out of the water, they like to hide under leaf piles and rotting logs. When feeling threatened, they release a toxin from their skin. (Not harmful to humans unless eaten). They also like to show their colorful bellies when a predator is near to warn them.

They have spiny looking bumps on their backs and can be bright green to a grayish brown. They normally reach about 2 inches in length. Their diet consists of worms and insects.
